Examples of birds



Eagle is the common name for the golden eagle,
bald eagle, and other birds of prey in the family Accipitridae.
Eagles belong to several groups of genera, some of which are closely related.
True eagles comprise the genus Aquila. Most of the 68 species of eagles
are from Eurasia and Africa.

Parrots, also known as psittacines,
are birds with a strong curved beak, upright stance, and clawed feet.
They are conformed by four families that contain roughly 410 species in 101 genera,
found mostly in tropical and subtropical regions.

Kingfishers are a family, the Alcedinidae,
of small to medium-sized, brightly coloured birds in the order Coraciiformes.
They have a cosmopolitan distribution, with most species living in the tropical
regions of Africa, Asia, and Oceania,
but also can be seen in Europe.

Gulls, or colloquially seagulls, are seabirds of the family
Laridae in the suborder Lari. They are most closely related to the terns and
skimmers and only distantly related to auks, and even more distantly to waders.
